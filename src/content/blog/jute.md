### **Soil Type & Preparation**

- Soil pH: **6.0 - 7.5** (neutral to slightly acidic).  
- Rich organic matter is essential for **better fiber yield**.

- Jute needs **consistent moisture** but cannot withstand **water stagnation**.  
- Requires **regular watering** in dry conditions.  
- Drip irrigation is **ideal** for better water management.

### **Step 7: Harvesting Jute**  
- **Jute is harvested after 4-5 months**, when plants are **8-12 feet tall**.  
- Cut plants **at the base**, leaving **roots in the soil**.  
- **Do not delay harvesting**, as overripe jute becomes coarse and brittle.

## **3. Post-Harvest Processing**  

### **Step 1: Retting (Fiber Extraction)**  
- Submerge harvested jute stalks in **slow-moving water** for **10-15 days**.  
- Retting helps **break down the soft tissue**, leaving only fibers.  
- After retting, **strip fibers manually** from the stalks.  

### **Step 2: Drying & Storage**  
- **Wash fibers thoroughly** to remove dirt.  
- Dry under **shade or indirect sunlight** for **2-3 days**.  
- Store in **a dry, cool place** to prevent moisture damage.

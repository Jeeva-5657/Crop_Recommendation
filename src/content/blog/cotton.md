Cotton (*Gossypium spp.*) is a **fiber crop** widely cultivated for its soft, fluffy fibers used in textiles. It is a **warm-season plant** that thrives in **sunny and dry climates**. Growing cotton at home is possible with the right soil, temperature, and care.  

---

## **1. Understanding Cotton Cultivation Requirements**  

### **Climate & Temperature**  
- Thrives in **warm, dry, and sunny climates**.  
- Ideal temperature: **21-37Â°C (70-100Â°F)**.  
- Requires **long growing seasons (150-180 days frost-free)**.  
- Cannot tolerate **frost or excessive moisture**.  

### **Soil Type & Preparation**  
- Prefers **well-drained, loamy, or sandy soil**.  
- Soil pH: **5.8 - 7.5** (slightly acidic to neutral).  
- Enrich soil with **organic matter or compost** for better fertility.  
- Avoid **heavy clay soil** to prevent root rot.  

### **Water Requirements**  
- Requires **moderate watering**, about **1-1.5 inches per week**.  
- Avoid **overwatering** as cotton is **drought-tolerant**.  
- Drip irrigation is ideal for moisture control.

### **Step 2: Seed Selection & Germination**  
- **Buy quality cotton seeds** from a local nursery or online store.  
- Soak seeds in **warm water for 6-8 hours** to speed up germination.  
- Germinate seeds indoors in **small containers** or **directly in the field**.  
- Cotton seeds germinate best at **18-30Â°C (65-85Â°F)**.  
- Sprouting occurs in **7-14 days**.  

---

### **Step 3: Planting Cotton Seeds**  

#### **Direct Sowing in the Garden**  
1. **Best time to plant**: Early **spring** after the last frost.  
2. **Spacing**: Plant **seeds 1 inch deep** and **4-6 inches apart**.  
3. **Row spacing**: Maintain **30-36 inches (2-3 feet) between rows**.  
4. **Soil Preparation**: Add **compost or aged manure** before planting.  
5. **Mulching**: Helps retain soil moisture and control weeds.  

#### **Growing in Containers**  
- Choose a **large pot (at least 10-15 gallons)** with drainage holes.  
- Use a **well-draining potting mix with compost**.  
- Provide **full sunlight (6-8 hours per day)**.  

---

### **Step 4: Watering & Fertilization**  
- **Water deeply but infrequently** (once or twice a week).  
- Use **organic compost or balanced fertilizer (NPK 10-10-10)**.  
- **At flowering stage**, increase **potassium and phosphorus** for better fiber production.  

---

### **Step 7: Harvesting Cotton**  
- **Cotton bolls** are ready for harvest **when they turn brown and split open**.  
- Harvest **manually** by pulling cotton fibers from the bolls.  
- Avoid **harvesting too early**, as immature cotton has low fiber quality.

## **3. Post-Harvest Handling**  

### **Step 1: Cleaning & Processing**  
- Remove **seeds from cotton fibers** (ginning process).  
- Dry cotton fibers **in the sun** for a few days.  

### **Step 2: Storing Cotton**  
- Store in **dry, cool places** to prevent moisture damage.  
- Use **cotton bags or breathable containers** for storage.

## **5. FAQs**  

### **Q: How long does cotton take to grow?**  
- Cotton takes **5-6 months** from planting to harvest.  

### **Q: Can cotton be grown in pots?**  
- Yes, but ensure a **large pot (at least 10-15 gallons)** and **full sun**.  

### **Q: Why are my cotton plants not flowering?**  
- Possible reasons: **Lack of sunlight, overwatering, or excess nitrogen**.  

### **Q: How many cotton bolls does one plant produce?**  
- A single plant can produce **10-20 cotton bolls**.
